## Overview

TSMC fabricates the large majority of the world's sub-7 nm logic. FY2024 revenue
was **$90.08B** (+30% YoY) on **$29.76B** capex, at a **56.1%** gross margin
([TSMC Q4 2024](/models/silicon-stack/sources/tsmc-q4-2024-results/)).

## The economics

- A **3nm 300 mm wafer sells for ~$18,000–20,000** ([Tom's Hardware](/models/silicon-stack/sources/toms-3nm-wafer/)); the blank substrate it starts from is **~$200** — see the [Idiot Index](/models/silicon-stack/idiot-index/).
- Advanced nodes (≤7 nm) are **~70% of revenue**; 3 nm alone went 6% → 25% in ~2.5 years.
- Bare manufacturing cost is well below the sale price (CSET modeled a 5nm wafer at ~$17k sale = ~$4,235 capital + the rest cost/markup).

## Energy & water

- **25.55 TWh** of electricity in 2024 (~9% of all Taiwan), ~13% renewable ([power data](/models/silicon-stack/sources/semivision-tsmc-power-2024/)) — ≈ **2,000 kWh per wafer** company-average (leading-edge higher).
- ~**8.3 m³ water/wafer** (5.7 m³ ultrapure), **>80% recycled** ([sustainability report](/models/silicon-stack/sources/tsmc-sustainability-2024/)).

## Position in the chain

Consumes [wafers](/models/silicon-stack/inputs/) (Shin-Etsu), EUV tools ([ASML](/models/silicon-stack/companies/asml/)), and gases; supplies dies + CoWoS packaging to fabless designers like [NVIDIA](/models/silicon-stack/companies/nvidia/).
