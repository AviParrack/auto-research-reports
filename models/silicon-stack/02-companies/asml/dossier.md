## Overview

ASML is the **sole supplier** of EUV lithography — the single hardest chokepoint
in the leading-edge stack. FY2025: **€32.7B** revenue, **€9.6B** net income,
52.8% gross margin, with **€8.2B** of recurring service revenue
([ASML Q4 2025](/models/silicon-stack/sources/asml-q4-2025-results/)).

## The machines

- **48 EUV systems** had revenue recognized in 2025 ([Counterpoint](/models/silicon-stack/sources/asml-2025-counterpoint/)).
- **Low-NA ~$180M**, **High-NA ~$380M** per system ([Tom's Hardware](/models/silicon-stack/sources/toms-highna-380m/)).
- A scanner is **~85% bought-in**: Zeiss optics (ASML invested ~$1B), Cymer EUV source (acquired ~$2.5B in 2013), Trumpf drive laser ([The Generalist](/models/silicon-stack/sources/generalist-asml/)).

## Per-wafer cost

SemiAnalysis models **~$30–70 per wafer per EUV layer**; a leading-edge wafer sees ~10–25 EUV layers ⇒ ~$300–1,500 of scanner-amortized EUV cost ([SemiAnalysis](/models/silicon-stack/sources/semianalysis-highna-cost/)). It reaches the wafer via tool depreciation inside TSMC's wafer price.

## Position in the chain

Integrates [Zeiss](/models/silicon-stack/companies/zeiss/)/Cymer/Trumpf into scanners sold to [TSMC](/models/silicon-stack/companies/tsmc/). See [Inputs → EUV lithography](/models/silicon-stack/inputs/).
