## Abstract

Air Liquide's 24 June 2024 announcement of its Baytown, Texas, low-carbon oxygen production facility provides a concrete tier-C anchor for the capex of large-scale ASU capacity. The facility will be built at ExxonMobil's Gulf Coast site (Baytown TX), comprising four large modular air-separation units with combined capacity of 9,000 metric tons of oxygen per day — a 50% increase in Air Liquide's Texas capacity. Capital expenditure is up to $850 million USD. The investment is conditional on policy and regulatory milestones tied to ExxonMobil's adjacent low-carbon hydrogen project.

## Key claims

- baytown-asu-capacity: "9,000 metric tons per day of oxygen production" (= 3.285 Mt/yr if continuous).
- baytown-asu-capex: "up to 850 million dollars" total capex.
- texas-capacity-step-up: Adds "50% increase in Air Liquide's Texas capacity" — implies prior Texas Air Liquide capacity was ~18 kt/d O2, total post-build ~27 kt/d.
- modular-asu-design: "four Large Modular Air separation units" — modular scaling pattern.
