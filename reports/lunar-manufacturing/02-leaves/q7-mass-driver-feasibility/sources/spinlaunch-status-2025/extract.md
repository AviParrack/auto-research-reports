## Abstract

SpinLaunch is the most-funded modern kinetic-launch company. Founded 2014 by Jonathan Yaney, $150M+ raised by 2022 (Kleiner Perkins, GV, Airbus Ventures), one-third-scale test accelerator operating at 20% of design capacity, demonstrated 30,000 ft suborbital altitudes. Full operational design target: 2.1 km/s (4,700 mph), 400 kg payload, 10,000 g peak. August 2025 demo: 1U CubeSat survived 10,000 g acceleration. May 2024 CEO change (Jonathan Yaney → David Wrenn). April 2025 strategic pivot away from kinetic launch as primary path — announced Meridian, a 280-satellite constellation to be launched conventionally on chemical rockets. August 2025 funding ($30M) supports Meridian, not the kinetic launcher. Successful testing of the 100-meter accelerator "expected by 2027." This is the most relevant operational Earth-based analogue for mass-driver-relevant engineering progress, and the trajectory is sobering: a decade of development, $150M+ capital, payload survives 10,000g but the operational orbital system is not built. Tier C as trade-press / company-press reference.

## Key claims

- founding: "Founded in 2014 by Jonathan Yaney, headquartered in Long Beach, California."
- design-target: "Full operational system is designed to spin rockets at speeds of up to 4,700 mph (7,500 km/h; 2.1 km/s)."
- payload-and-g: "Maximum payload: 880 lbs (400 kg) per launch. Peak centrifugal acceleration: approximately 10,000 G's."
- august-2025-demo: "Pivotal milestone where the company accelerated a 1U CubeSat prototype to 10,000 Gs — equivalent to 10,000 times Earth's gravity — demonstrating that satellites can survive the extreme forces of its centrifugal launch system."
- pivot-2025: "April 2025 strategic pivot... announced Meridian, a 280-satellite constellation to be launched conventionally on chemical rockets, while still planning to develop kinetic launch systems."
- capital-and-timeline: "$150 million raised by September 2022. $30 million 2025 round for Meridian. Successful testing of the 100-meter accelerator expected by 2027."
- testing-gap: "Hasn't conducted a single test since September 2022, representing over two years of radio silence on the testing front" before the August 2025 demo.

## Reviewer notes

Tier C as company / trade press reference. Important Earth-based analogue for mass-driver engineering: a private kinetic-launcher company with $150M+ capital, world-class investors, and a decade of development has demonstrated 10,000g survival but not 2.1 km/s muzzle velocity nor operational orbital insertion. The strategic pivot to chemical rockets for their satellite constellation is a meaningful signal that even their own engineering team views the kinetic path as longer than expected. For q7 capital-buildup analysis: the SpinLaunch trajectory suggests that a private-capital-only path to operational kinetic launch is slow and uncertain — meaningful state involvement (DoD EMRG, NASA NIAC) has historically been the actual driver of progress. Cycle-life and capital are the constraints, not physics. Cross-references the Navy EMRG cancellation (cycle-life failure after ~$500M invested).
