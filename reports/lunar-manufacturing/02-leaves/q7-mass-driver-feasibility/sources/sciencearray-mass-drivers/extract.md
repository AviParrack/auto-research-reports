## Abstract

Science Array is a popular-science / trade-press space-economy blog. Their lunar mass driver overview compiles canonical figures across the literature: velocity requirements (1.7 km/s orbital, 2.4 km/s escape vs Earth's 11.2 km/s), Navy EMALS as the closest operational analogue (30-ton aircraft to flying speed in <2 seconds), Tel Aviv University 40 kg prototype, megawatts continuous operation, temperature envelope -173 to 127°C. Cost projection: "Could potentially launch material for under $100 per kilogram - possibly as low as $10 per kilogram with economies of scale," versus current Earth launch at $2,000-$10,000/kg. Timeline: "First-generation systems: 2040s at earliest; 2030s initial lunar industrial operations; 2050s multiple mass drivers." This is the headline trade-press synthesis our q2.c5 references for the "$50/kg late-era" framing.

## Reviewer notes

Tier C trade press. The $10-100/kg "with economies of scale" figure is the most-cited optimistic anchor in the trade-press literature; our q2.c5 derives a comparable $50/kg late-era figure from first principles. The 2040s/2050s calendar timeline runs into anti-pattern #11 (naive calendar timelines) — these are projections under business-as-usual; under TAI-grade acceleration the timeline compresses dramatically, under a stall it never arrives. Useful for triangulating the "target" the field aspires to, but the specific calendar years should be replaced with step-count + acceleration-sensitivity in our write pass.
