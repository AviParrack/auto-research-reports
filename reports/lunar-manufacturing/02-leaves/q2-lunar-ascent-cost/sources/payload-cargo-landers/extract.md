# Payload Space — cargo lander specs

Trade-press confirmation of cargo lunar lander payload capacities for the chemical-rocket scenarios.

## payload-capacities-cargo-variants

- **SpaceX Starship HLS Cargo:** 12–15 metric tons of payload (NASA statement, April 2024). Note: this is for the *cargo variant* contract, distinct from the crewed HLS variant's 100 t maximum surface payload.
- **Blue Origin Blue Moon Mark 2 Cargo:** 12–15 metric tons of payload
- **Blue Origin Blue Moon Mark 1:** 3 metric tons to lunar surface (smaller variant, targeting 2025 launch on New Glenn)

## timeline

- Starship lunar cargo delivery scheduled for Artemis VII in 2032
- Blue Moon Mark 1 prioritized for near-term operational deployment

## cost-figures

No explicit cost figures provided in this article.

## relevance-to-q2

The cargo-variant payload of 12–15 t (rather than the crewed-HLS 100 t headline) is more realistic for the *operational* near-term lunar surface delivery scenario. Recalculating with this anchor:

If Starship HLS Cargo delivers 12 t to lunar surface per mission, and per-mission cost is $400–600M (per newspaceeconomy.ca trade press), then the **Earth-imports-only lunar surface delivery cost is $33,000–50,000/kg** in early Starship era. This is the operational reality, distinct from the Block-2-mature 100 t headline number.

For lunar surface to LEO (reverse direction), this cost roughly doubles or worse — every kg coming back from the Moon requires the lander to carry its own ascent propellant, which was itself launched from Earth at terrestrial cost.

## limitations

- 12–15 t cargo variant is the *current contracted* value; mature Block 2 Starship cargo could exceed this
- Timeline (2032 for Artemis VII Starship cargo) suggests this scenario doesn't begin operationally until late in the q2 era band
- No direct cost figure; cost extrapolated from companion newspaceeconomy.ca source
