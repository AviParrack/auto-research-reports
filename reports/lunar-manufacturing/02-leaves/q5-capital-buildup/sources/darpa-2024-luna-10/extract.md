## Abstract

The DARPA 10-Year Lunar Architecture (LunA-10) Capability Study presents initial results from a seven-month integrated-architecture study completed in 2024. The study spans 14 industrial partners (Blue Origin, CisLunar Industries, Crescent Space Services, Fibertek, Firefly Aerospace, GITAI, Helios, Honeybee Robotics, ICON, Nokia of America, Northrop Grumman, Redwire, Sierra Space, SpaceX) across six focus areas: communications and navigation; construction and robotics; market analysis; mining and in-situ resource utilization; power; and transit, mobility and logistics. The study identifies the central gap in lunar development as "fractionation" — isolated, self-sufficient systems — and proposes "connectivity" via shared infrastructure as the architectural fix. The framework outlines four notional "Lunar Ages" and five enterprise value chains. Emerging concepts requiring further development include recyclable in-situ resource utilization (Re-ISRU), robotics-as-a-service (RaaS), and aggregated thermal generation and rejection services. Initial Phase 1 contracts to selected companies were capped at $1M each. No total integrated-architecture cost is disclosed in the public release.

## Reviewer notes

Load-bearing for q5: provides the most authoritative current US-government framing of "what does a connected lunar architecture look like" and an explicit list of the 14 companies whose roadmaps shape the public discussion. Treated as Tier A because while DARPA is a primary-source agency, this is an integration-study summary rather than a primary engineering paper.
