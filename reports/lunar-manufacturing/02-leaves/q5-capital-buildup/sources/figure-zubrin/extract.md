## Abstract

Robert Zubrin is the canonical Mars-first credentialed engineer-commentator. He is fundamentally skeptical of lunar Gateway, lunar manufacturing as a Mars-precursor, and what he sees as program-of-record cost bloat. His "Moon Direct" proposal estimates the cost of initial lunar missions at $1.5 billion, followed by a yearly cost of $420 million to keep things going — a dramatic compression vs the OIG-projected $93B Artemis cost. The proposal uses Falcon Heavy-derived cargo landers delivering 10 tonnes of payload to the lunar surface, with electrolyzed lunar ice as in-situ propellant for both return vehicles and surface mobility. He frames Gateway as adding cost, risk, and timing constraints for no engineering benefit.

## Key statements

- moon-direct-1-5b-initial: "The cost of the initial missions to get things started at $1.5 billion"
- moon-direct-420m-annual: "Followed by a yearly cost of $420 million to keep things going"
- gateway-fortune-cost: "[Gateway] will cost a fortune to build, a fortune to maintain, and it will add to the cost, risk, and timing constraints of all subsequent missions to the moon or Mars by adding an unnecessary stop along the way"
- moon-direct-10t-payload: "10 tons of payload to the lunar surface" via Falcon Heavy-derived cargo landers
- lunar-ice-isru-propellant: "Such ice could be electrolyzed to make hydrogen-oxygen rocket propellant, to fuel both Earth-return vehicles as well as flying rocket vehicles"
- mars-priority-skeptical-of-lunar-stepping-stone: "Neither [asteroid nor Moon] would be able to provide all of its own oxygen, water, or energy; these resources are producible on Mars"

## Reviewer notes

Tier-B credentialed engineer-advocate; offers the lowest-end published dollar figure for "minimal sustained lunar presence" ($1.5B + $420M/yr). Zubrin's numbers should be treated as direct-architectural lower bound — they assume no manufacturing capacity (just propellant + crew), no industrial export, no extended habitat beyond direct-mission. So Zubrin's figures are not actually "net-positive export lunar manufacturing base" capex — they are "minimal sustained lunar presence" capex, which is a different milestone. Useful as the absolute floor of credentialed-engineer estimates. To be reviewed in sub-pass 4.
