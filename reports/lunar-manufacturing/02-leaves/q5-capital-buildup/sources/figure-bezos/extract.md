## Abstract

Jeff Bezos has framed the long-term move to lunar and cislunar manufacturing as the only way to sustainably continue economic growth: heavy industry must move off Earth and the Moon, with its proximity and resources, is the staging ground. He articulated this at the May 2019 Blue Moon unveiling and in subsequent statements. The long-term vision is O'Neill-style cislunar habitats supporting up to 1 trillion humans. Near-term: the Blue Moon lander is sized to deliver up to 6.5 metric tons of cargo to the lunar surface in its largest configuration. Bezos has not offered total capex figures publicly but his deployment of multibillion-dollar personal capital into Blue Origin is the de-facto investment signal.

## Key statements

- time-to-go-back-to-stay: "It's time to go back to the moon, this time to stay" (May 2019 Blue Moon unveiling)
- road-to-space: "We're going to build a road to space. And then, amazing things will happen" (May 2019)
- trillion-people-vision: "Not just millions of people in space but eventually 1 trillion, living not on moons or planets, but space colonies in a style originally envisioned by a Princeton physicist, Gerard K. O'Neill"
- heavy-industry-off-earth: "To sustainably offer humankind the chance for growth, heavy industry has to move off the surface of Earth and rely on resources from the rest of the solar system, such as the minerals and water ice in the lunar south pole's Shackleton crater"
- blue-moon-65-tonnes: "Blue Moon is propelled by liquid hydrogen and can precisely land up to 6.5 metric tons of cargo on the moon's surface in its largest configuration"
