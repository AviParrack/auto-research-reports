## Abstract

CSIS analysis arguing there is "little sign of a business case for cislunar activities that is not closely tied to government funding," and "no compelling strategic military value from cislunar space" within the next decade for potential conflicts. The case is structural: GPS is unreliable in cislunar regions; time operates differently on the Moon (56 microseconds faster daily due to relativity); three-body orbital mechanics require new computational models; insufficient space situational awareness infrastructure; unresolved international governance frameworks. Exception noted: space situational awareness capabilities may offer near-term national-security benefits.

## Key claims

- no-private-business-case: "There is little sign of a business case for cislunar activities that is not closely tied to government funding"
- no-strategic-military-value: "No compelling strategic military value from cislunar space within the next decade for potential conflicts with China, Russia, or other adversaries"
- gps-cislunar-unreliable: "GPS is unreliable in cislunar regions without enhancement"
- relativistic-time-offset: "Time operates differently on the Moon (56 microseconds faster daily due to relativity)"
- exception-ssa: "Space situational awareness capabilities may offer national security benefits"
