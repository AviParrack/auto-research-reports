## Abstract

Bill Nelson, NASA Administrator 2021-2025, set the policy frame for the current Artemis-plus-Moon-base program of record. His public statements emphasized urgency vs China and a $4.2B per-launch cost estimate for SLS/Orion. Nelson framed Artemis 2 (Sept 2025) and Artemis 3 (Sept 2026) under fixed-price SpaceX contracting. His China-cost-comparison commentary noted Beijing's claimed $8B/yr civilian space budget is widely considered an undercount because the military space program is embedded in undisclosed military spending.

## Key statements

- 4-2b-per-launch: "The current cost estimate per launch in the Artemis mission is $4.2 billion"
- artemis-2-sept-2025: "Artemis 2 is planned for September 2025, when a crew of three Americans and a Canadian will circle the moon"
- artemis-3-sept-2026: "Artemis 3 (to land astronauts on the moon) scheduled for September 2026 under a fixed-price contract with SpaceX"
- china-not-first: When asked if China could beat the US back to the moon: "Not if I have anything to say about it, and not if Joe Biden has anything to say about it"
- china-8b-undercount: "Beijing claims its space program is funded at just $8 billion a year, but most people consider that an extreme lowball because their space program is embedded in their military, and their military budget is not disclosed"
- south-pole-concern: Nelson publicly expressed concern about China getting to the south pole first and staking a claim
