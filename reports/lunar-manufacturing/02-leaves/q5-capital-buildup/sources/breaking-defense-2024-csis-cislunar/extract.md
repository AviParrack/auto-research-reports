## Abstract

Breaking Defense coverage of the October 22, 2024 CSIS Aerospace Security Project analysis by Clayton Swope and Louis Gleason. The analysis argues no compelling strategic military value from cislunar space in the next decade and notes that GPS unreliability, relativistic time offsets (~56 μs/day faster on the Moon), three-body orbital mechanics, weak space situational awareness, and unresolved governance frameworks all degrade the near-term commercial and military case. Treated as a Tier C container for the CSIS analysis (csis-swope-2024-cislunar).

## Key claims

- csis-no-business-case: "Little sign of a business case for cislunar activities that is not closely tied to government funding"
- csis-no-military-value: "No compelling strategic military value from cislunar space within the next decade for potential conflicts"
- ssa-exception: "Space situational awareness capabilities may offer national security benefits"
