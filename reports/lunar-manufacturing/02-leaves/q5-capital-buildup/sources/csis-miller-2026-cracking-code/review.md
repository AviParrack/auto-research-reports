# Source Review: CSIS Miller 2026 — Cracking the Code

**Overall verdict:** mixed
**Two-sentence summary:** A policy-advocacy piece arguing for an international Lunar Development Authority modeled on the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey or CERN. Useful for institutional framing but offers no direct lunar-capex numbers; the benchmarks given (Earth real estate $400T, global construction $15T/yr, PA-NYNJ $9.4B, Houston PW $3.5B) are scale-anchors, not capex projections.

## Key claims

- "Lunar Development Authority modeled on PA-NYNJ and CERN" — Consistent (institutional structure). Aligns with our calc's framing of the lunar economy as multi-party / multi-decade — the LDA structure is the institutional plumbing for a $72-88B all-party cumulative spend.
- "Annual global construction spending exceeds $15 trillion" — Consistent (benchmark). Useful as scale-context: a $20-200B lunar-buildup over a decade is small compared to global construction; the question is "what makes lunar work different," not "is lunar economically too large."
- "PA-NYNJ annual budget: $9.4B; Houston PW: $3.5B" — Consistent. Direct evidence that the institutional precedent (a port authority) operates at a similar dollar scale to what we'd need for a lunar infrastructure authority.
- "There have yet to be significant nongovernmental revenues for lunar activities" — Consistent (with MacDonald CSIS view). Reinforces the pessimistic-commercial-revenue framing.
- "The Moon as a real estate development project anchored by public-private partnerships" — Merits investigation (frame). Useful framing but not a capex estimate.
