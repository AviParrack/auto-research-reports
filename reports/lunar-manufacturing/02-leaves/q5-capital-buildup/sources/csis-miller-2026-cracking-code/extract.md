## Abstract

A CSIS analysis arguing the US should treat the Moon as a "real estate development project anchored by public-private partnerships and an international Lunar Development Authority (LDA)." Miller proposes the LDA be chartered and led by the US, modeled on the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey (PA-NYNJ) and CERN. The piece emphasises that to be enduring, lunar presence must be independent of US taxpayer reliance. Comparative scales offered: Earth's real-estate value ~$400 trillion; annual global construction spending exceeds $15 trillion; PA-NYNJ's annual budget is $9.4 billion; Houston Public Works' annual budget is $3.5 billion. These benchmarks are presented to show that lunar economic scale is plausible relative to specific terrestrial precedent. The analysis is institutional/structural rather than capex-quantitative.
