## Abstract

NASA OIG audit IG-22-003 (November 15 2021) projected $93 billion in NASA spending on the Artemis program through fiscal year 2025, covering development from FY2012 onward. The report estimated SLS/Orion production cost at ~$4.1 billion per launch (Artemis 1-3 architecture). The audit issued nine recommendations for cost capture, reporting, and reduction; Associate Administrator Jim Free (Exploration Systems Development Mission Directorate) was the agency interlocutor. The OIG flagged the unsustainability of the per-launch cost: "without capturing, accurately reporting and reducing the cost of future SLS/Orion missions, the Agency will face significant challenges." Apollo program comparison: ~$28B between 1960-1973 (roughly $280B in 2021 dollars).

## Reviewer notes

NASA OIG is the de-facto authoritative source for Artemis program-level cost reporting. Load-bearing for q5: $93B / 13 years is the program-of-record dollar anchor for "this is what we have actually spent / will have spent through FY2025." Note that this is the *cislunar transport and crewed-mission* cost — it does NOT include any of the surface-manufacturing capex (ISRU plants, mass driver, lunar power, lunar manufacturing equipment). For q5 buildup, $93B is the cost of "getting humans repeatedly to lunar orbit and surface" — the architectural backbone on top of which manufacturing capex is added. Pair with spaceflightnow-2026-20b-moonbase (Isaacman's $20B over 7yr surface-base announcement) for the most current architectural reframe: NASA cancelled Gateway, will redirect components to surface base, target $20B incremental over 7 years for surface infrastructure.
