## Abstract

In a TIME magazine cover interview (March 2026), Gwynne Shotwell, SpaceX President & COO, articulated SpaceX's lunar manufacturing direction. Headline statements: humans on the lunar surface before 2030; lunar manufacturing of AI satellites within ~5 years; lunar settlement plus manufacturing base "within the next decade." The lower lunar gravity is positioned as the structural advantage making this economically attractive vs Earth-launched satellites. The framing is openly aspirational but distinguishes the company's actual investment priorities from purely promotional Musk-style tweets. Shotwell pairs the lunar manufacturing thesis with continued Starship development cadence.

## Key statements

- humans-before-2030: "I think we will put humans on the surface before 2030, yes"
- lunar-manufacturing-would-not-surprise: When asked about doing manufacturing on the moon: "Yes, that would not surprise me if we did that"
- lunar-settlement-decade: "Lunar settlement and manufacturing base within the next decade — ideally in five years — where AI satellites could be built using materials mined on the lunar surface"
- lower-gravity-advantage: "The Moon's lower gravity, she noted, would make launches from there far more efficient than from Earth"
