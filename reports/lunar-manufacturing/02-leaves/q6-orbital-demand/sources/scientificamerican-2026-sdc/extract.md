## Abstract

Scientific American analysis of the orbital data center business case.
Data centers projected to constitute "nearly half of U.S. electricity
demand growth" through 2030; global demand potentially doubles by end
of decade. Starcloud projects its facility to require 5 GW by 2035.
Google's Suncatcher initiative cites a $200/kg launch cost threshold
by 2035 as viability prerequisite. Comparative claims: Starcloud says
their solar-powered orbital DC could achieve "10× lower carbon
emissions" vs natural-gas-powered ground DC. Saarland University
researchers (referenced) find orbital DC could create "an order of
magnitude greater emissions than a data center on Earth" when launch
+ reentry emissions are factored in. Benjamin Lee (UPenn) cited as
expert skeptic.
