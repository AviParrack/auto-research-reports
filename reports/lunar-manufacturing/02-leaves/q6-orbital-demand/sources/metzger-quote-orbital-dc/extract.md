## Abstract

Metzger's public-record positions on orbital data center demand and
on space infrastructure scaling philosophy. Headline statement:
"A lot of the skepticism of data centers in space probably comes from
failing to price in the effects of exponential expansion. If AI
doesn't grow exponentially, then I don't think it will make sense
very soon to put them in space; but I think it will grow exponentially."
Adjacent statement on cislunar scaling: "Space infrastructure will
only scale when we treat it as infrastructure — not as a series of
one-off missions." Metzger is also the primary author of Metzger 2023
(the tier S source) — public-figure quote review covers only the
quoted statements, separate from the tier S paper review.

## Key claims

- exponential-conditional: "If AI doesn't grow exponentially, then I
  don't think it will make sense very soon to put them in space; but
  I think it will grow exponentially."
- pricing-skepticism: "A lot of the skepticism of data centers in
  space probably comes from failing to price in the effects of
  exponential expansion."
- infrastructure-not-missions: "Space infrastructure will only scale
  when we treat it as infrastructure — not as a series of one-off
  missions."
