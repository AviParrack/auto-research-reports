## Abstract

Pickard's analysis identifies data throughput as the principal
economic constraint on orbital data centers — comparable to the
regulatory and physical limitations on space-manufactured goods
returning to Earth. The post emphasizes that satellite-to-ground
communications are bottlenecked by line-of-sight passes ("the
satellite has to pass in the field of view of a ground station and
that can take time") and that orbital operators "still need access
to lots of real estate around the world" for ground stations. Pickard
does not provide quantitative mass, GW, or crossover projections;
the contribution is conceptual rather than numerical.

## Key claims

- bandwidth-binding-constraint: Data throughput is the primary
  bottleneck for orbital DC viability.
- ground-station-real-estate: Orbital operators still need terrestrial
  ground station infrastructure at scale.
- pass-latency: Single-satellite-to-ground passes are intermittent
  by orbital geometry.
