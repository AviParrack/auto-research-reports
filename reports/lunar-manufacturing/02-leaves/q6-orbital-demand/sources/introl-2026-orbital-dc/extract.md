## Abstract

Introl's February 2026 industry snapshot documents the state of the
orbital data center industry. Active companies with hardware in orbit:
Kepler Communications (10 optical relay satellites, January 2026, 300 kg
each); Axiom Space (2 nodes, January 2026); Starcloud (1× 60 kg
satellite with NVIDIA H100, November 2025; first orbital LLM training
December 2025). Mega-constellation filings: SpaceX/xAI January 2026
for up to 1M satellites at 500-2,000 km, projecting 100 GW AI compute
from 1M t/yr of satellite launches; Starcloud February 2026 for
88,000 satellites; Blue Origin Project Sunrise for ~52,000 satellites.
Market projection: $1.77B (2029) → $39.09B (2035) at 67.4% CAGR. Launch
threshold: $100/kg Starship target needed for cost parity with
terrestrial.

- launch-threshold: $100/kg LEO Starship target needed for
  cost-parity with terrestrial.

## Reviewer notes

Tier C: industry blog tracking commercial filings. The SpaceX
"1M t/yr → 100 GW" projection is the single most-quotable industrial-
explosion-regime demand figure for orbital data centers. Per-satellite
mass references (300 kg Kepler, 60 kg Starcloud-1) anchor the
per-satellite scaling. Note that all mega-constellation filings are
FCC paper filings, not committed launch contracts; treat as upper
bounds under aggressive deployment. Tier C scalar verdict applies.
