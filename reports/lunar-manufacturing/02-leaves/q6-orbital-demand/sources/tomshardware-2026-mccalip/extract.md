## Abstract

Tom's Hardware coverage of Andrew McCalip's (Varda) public SpaceGPT
calculator estimating orbital data center economics. McCalip's
headline finding: a 1 GW orbital data center would cost ~$51.1B over
5 years vs ~$15.9B for equivalent terrestrial — i.e., orbital is 3×
more expensive even under McCalip's "extremely optimistic projections"
of continued Starship cost reduction. McCalip characterizes the
orbital-DC enthusiasm as "FOMO and aesthetic futurism" rather than
defensible economics, and critiques the industry's analytical posture:
"people are using back-of-the-envelope math, doing a terrible job of
it, and only confirming whatever conclusion they already want."

## Key claims

- 1gw-orbital-51b: $51.1B over 5 years for 1 GW orbital data center
  per McCalip's calculator.
- 1gw-terrestrial-16b: $15.9B for equivalent terrestrial 1 GW data
  center.
- 3x-multiple: Orbital DC is "over 3× more expensive" under
  optimistic launch-cost assumptions.
- fomo-quote: McCalip characterizes orbital DC enthusiasm as "FOMO and
  aesthetic futurism."
- math-critique: McCalip: "people are using back-of-the-envelope math,
  doing a terrible job of it, and only confirming whatever conclusion
  they already want."
