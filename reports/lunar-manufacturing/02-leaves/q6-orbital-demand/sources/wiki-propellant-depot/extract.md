## Abstract

Wikipedia overview of orbital propellant depots. Key reference data
points used for orientation: NASA's 2011 Simple Depot concept stored
~30 t total propellant (5 t LH2 + 25.7 t LOX). SpaceX Starship tanker
carries ~100 t propellant per LEO flight; Musk estimates 8 launches
suffice to refill a Starship for interplanetary trajectory while NASA
estimates 16 due to cryogenic boiloff. ULA ACES (proposed, unfunded)
carries ~73 t. Former NASA admin Mike Griffin (2005): "at a
conservatively low government price of $10,000 per kg in LEO, 250
tonnes of fuel for two missions per year is worth $2.5 billion." Tier
E used for orientation; the Starship tanker and Mike Griffin figures
are re-cited from primary sources elsewhere in q6's source set.
