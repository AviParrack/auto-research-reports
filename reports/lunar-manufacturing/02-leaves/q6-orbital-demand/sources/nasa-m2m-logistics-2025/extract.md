## Abstract

NASA's February 2025 Lunar Logistics, Mobility, and Cargo white paper
extends the 2024 architecture review with updated cargo demand
projections for the Artemis-era. The document identifies cargo
delivery needs across initial-surface-habitat deployment, science
payload delivery, mobility hardware, and crew consumables. The
structural mass-budget gap relative to Artemis-V-and-beyond goals is
the central finding: current planned commercial cargo landers fill
only a fraction of projected need, and the gap motivates either new
lander development, Starship HLS-derived cargo variant, or substantial
program rescoping. The paper is part of the 2025 Architecture Concept
Review (ACR) cycle and informs subsequent program direction documents.

## Key claims

- updated-gap-2025: Cargo-delivery gap quantified across surface
  habitat deployment, mobility, science, and consumables for
  Artemis-V-and-beyond goals.
- new-element-cargo-lander: A new lunar surface cargo lander class
  passed mission concept review in 2024 and appears in this revision.
- initial-surface-habitat: Two new elements (initial surface habitat
  and lunar surface cargo lander) added since the 2024 ACR cycle.
- strategic-tradeoff: Three paths to close the cargo gap — develop a
  larger commercial cargo lander, use Starship HLS for cargo, or
  rescope program objectives.
