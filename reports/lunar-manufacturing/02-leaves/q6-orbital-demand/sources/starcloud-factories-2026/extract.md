## Abstract

Reference page documenting Starcloud's planned orbital data center
constellation. Starcloud (formerly Lumen Orbit) targets a 300-satellite
network in very low Earth orbit operating as an orbital data relay
constellation. Stated capacity target: gigawatt-scale orbital data
center system. Demonstrator (Starcloud-1) launched late 2025 with
"100× more powerful GPUs than have ever been operated in space" via
NVIDIA Inception partnership. Commercial Starcloud-2 plans
fully-operational sun-synchronous orbit by 2026. Long-term vision
(per company statements and FCC filings) extends to 88,000 satellites
and a 5 GW hypercluster.

## Key claims

- 300-satellite-near-term: 300-satellite orbital data relay
  constellation in VLEO.
- gw-target: Starcloud targets gigawatt-scale orbital data center
  capacity.
- starcloud-1-h100: Demonstrator launched late 2025 with NVIDIA H100
  GPU.
- 88000-sats-fcc: Starcloud FCC filing for 88,000 satellites.
- 5gw-hypercluster: Long-term vision: 5 GW hypercluster with 4 km²
  solar array.
