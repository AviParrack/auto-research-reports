# Falcon 9 reuse milestone — March 2026

**Headline:** B1076 booster set the Falcon 9 reuse record at **34 flights** (Starlink delivery mission, March 30, 2026).

**Booster history:** B1076 entered the fleet in 2021. Across 34 launches it flew CRS-22, Crew-3, Turksat 5B, Crew-4, CRS-25, Eutelsat Hotbird 13G, SES O3B mPOWER-A, PSN Satria, Telkomsat Merah Putih 2, Galileo L13, Koreasat-6A Crew-6, USSF-124, plus 22 Starlink batches.

**Not in this source:** Fleet-average reuse count. SpaceX's stated max target. Current refurbishment cost percentage.

## Relevance for q1 calc reuse-scenario boundaries

| Scenario | Reuses per stack | B1076 evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Optimistic (Musk target, aircraft-like) | 100 | Currently 3× above the demonstrated max |
| Partial | 30 | **Demonstrated** — B1076 already at 34 |
| Pessimistic (Falcon 9 fleet average circa 2024) | 10 | Below current max but possibly near fleet mean |

B1076 at 34 reuses provides direct evidence that the partial-scenario (30 reuses) is **operationally demonstrated** for Falcon 9 hardware, not aspirational. The optimistic 100-reuse scenario for Starship is roughly 3× beyond the demonstrated Falcon 9 envelope. Starship being larger and more complex makes the 100× target *harder* per dollar of build cost, but it's also designed with reusability primary from day one.

The fleet-average reuse count would refine the partial-vs-pessimistic boundary. Not available in this source.
