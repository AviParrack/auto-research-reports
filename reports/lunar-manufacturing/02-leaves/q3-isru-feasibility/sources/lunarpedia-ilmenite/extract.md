# Ilmenite Reduction (Lunarpedia)

## reaction-chemistry

Reduction step: `FeTiO3 + H2 → Fe + TiO2 + H2O`

Water splitting: `2 H2O → 2 H2 + O2`

Net reaction: `2 FeTiO3 → 2 Fe + 2 TiO2 + O2`

Products: free iron, titanium dioxide (TiO2 byproduct), and oxygen via
water-splitting; hydrogen is recovered for re-use.

## temperature

Temperature documented as "1050°C" for lunar ilmenite reduction (the
caption-referenced figure).

## trl-status

"Hydrogen reduction is one method currently being tested by many Universities"
— no explicit TRL number on Lunarpedia.

(Cross-reference: NASA Sanders 2025 indicates the broader hydrogen-reduction
family at TRL 5-6 for equatorial sites; see lyon-industries-isru-2026 and
nasa-sanders-2025.)

## lunar-siting-constraint

"This process is best utilized if the plant is sited in a location in which
ilmenite composes a high fraction of the soil."

Ilmenite content is highest in **mare** regions (basaltic, Fe-Ti-rich); in
highland anorthositic regolith, ilmenite is typically below 2 vol% (cross-ref
arxiv-simulant-2601 and wustl-lunar-soil).

## earth-imports

Hydrogen is the working gas; in steady state it is recycled via water
electrolysis. Initial hydrogen charge and makeup must be imported until polar
water becomes available.
