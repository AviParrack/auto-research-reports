# Physicochemical Properties of Lunar Regolith Simulant for ISRU Oxygen Production

## simulant-mineralogy

**LHS-1 / LHS-2 (Lunar Highlands Simulants):**
- Anorthosite 74.4 wt%
- Glass-rich basalt 24.7 wt%
- Ilmenite 0.4 wt%
- Olivine 0.2 wt%
- Pyroxene 0.3 wt%

**LSP-2 (Lunar South Pole simulant):**
- Anorthosite 90.0 wt%
- Glass-rich basalt 10.0 wt%

## elemental-composition-lhs2

LHS-2 (highlands proxy): O 61.5%, Si 23.8%, Al 7.6%, Ca 5.2%, K 1.0%, Mg 0.8%
(by weight).

(Note: O at 61.5% is anomalously high for a *bulk* lunar composition; this
appears to be the *oxide-stripped O* count for the silicate matrix in this
specific simulant. Real lunar regolith bulk O is closer to 41-45% per
wustl-lunar-soil and Wikipedia.)

## ilmenite-yield-h2-reduction

"Ilmenite produces an apparent yield of 1.10 wt% oxygen under hydrogen
reduction conditions" at 900°C via:

`FeTiO3 + H2 → Fe + TiO2 + H2O`

## highland-yield-h2-reduction

"Highland simulant (LHS-2) achieved only approximately 0.02 wt% oxygen
extraction despite low ilmenite, attributed to distributed Fe-bearing silicate
and glassy phases."

## south-pole-yield-h2-reduction

LSP-2 yielded only "0.01 wt%" oxygen.

## process-implication

"Oxygen extraction behavior in realistic lunar regolith is governed by
whole-regolith response rather than ilmenite content alone, supporting
processing strategies beyond ilmenite-selective beneficiation for
highland/polar regions."

In plain English: hydrogen reduction is a mare/Fe-rich process. For
highland/polar regolith, MRE or carbothermal (which process the whole
silicate matrix) are necessary.
